Let them hate, so long as they fear

B

lack March produces dark and aggressive music inspired by the thought that human existence and beliefs draw from it are abominations. In 2012, Black March releases its first song with the demo title A new Order. With a solid line-up, the band starts playing live in the end of 2013 and releases its first demo Oderint Dum Metuant in April 2014. The latter offers a conventional but really effective Black Metal, combining harshness and atmosphere mixed with the intensity of Thrash Metal.

Lots of work has been done since, many shows performed, the first album Praeludium Exterminii was digitally released on May 2017, and the EP Gloria In Ruinam is now available since the fall of 2020. Black March is looking forward to spread its hatred music all over the world.

A solid debut of tight and intense thrashy black metal

Black March are not ones to muck about with meandering instrumental passages or self-indulgent lead guitar meanderings: their songs get to the point in a matter of minutes with good guitar riffs, a solid and stern bass, and punishing, even booming percussion.

—Encyclopaedia Metallum: The Metal Archives
